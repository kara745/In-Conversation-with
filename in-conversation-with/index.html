Design Student Insight

Matt Mancini

An interview by Kara Ross

Kara—Why do you want to be a designer?

Matt—I actually don’t want to pursue a career in Graphic Design. When I finish this program, however, i will use these skills to benefit my businesses.

Kara—What makes you nervous about becoming a designer?

Matt—If i were to become a designer, i would be nervous about meeting clients because i’m not a social person.

Kara—What excites you about design?

Matt—What excites me about design is vector work. I love computers and I love working in most computer programs. I also enjoy video editing, and hopefully we will get into some of that in the semesters to come.

Kara—What are your strengths as a designer?

Matt—My strengths are definitely vector artwork. I have always been good with computers and computer interfaces and my knowledge of everything has been a benefit in this program.

Kara—What are some areas of opportunity to work on?

Matt—I can work on sharpening my photoshop skills and my illustration work. I’m not a drawer/illustrator and sometimes I have to get out of my comfort zone, that’s the only way i’ll learn.

Kara—What aspects of graphic design do you gravitate towards?

Matt—I gravite towards computer work and the Adobe programs. I prefer to use Adobe illustrator because that is the program that i first learned when starting this program and now I have become connected with it and learned a lot.

Kara—What are the roles and responsibilities of a graphic designer in society?

Matt—The roles and responsibilities of a graphic designer are; to be aware of past and current themes, what’s happening in the world present day, and to always do your best work because your designs will be out for the world to see and may have impact on others.

Kara—When/where/why are you at your most creative/productive?

Matt—I’m at my most creative/productive when i work at home on my dining room table, listening to music. I feel comfortable and ready to get a lot done, especially in this second year!

Kara—What are your comfort levels with giving and receiving feedback?

Matt—I don’t like giving feedback because I dont want to ofend anyone in any way, however, even though I dont like talking in a quiet room with a group of people listening to me, if the person and I were having a 1 on 1 critque, then I will give them my full opinion regardless.

Kara—What is a website?

Matt—I see a website like an information book for a computer. It’s a digital place where people can read about the given information and can bookmark/save any website they want.

Kara—Why did you choose to come into this program?

Matt—I chose this program because I enjoy computers and creating things, especially vector work. Coming into this program, I thought that everything was done on the computer, until I met James. If it wasn’t for my parents forcing me to go to post secondary, I would have started working full time in my construction business and vending machine business.

Kara—Who do you look up to in Graphic Design (inspiration)?

Matt—Honestly, I don’t have an inspiration in Graphic Design, however, if I were to chose two people they would be Dereck from Schedule 2. His vector work is amazing and his love for design is strong. The second person would be Ian McDonald. I would choose Ian because what he is teaching us in Web Design is fascinating and his style in building websites is what I want to absorb from his class.

Kara—So far, which course has been your favourite?

Matt—So far my favourite course has been Ian’s Web Design course. Ironic that this survey is for his class, however, coding and building websites with dropdowns and animations and all this cool stuff, really excites me and it’s a class I look forward to going to during the week.

Kara—What are some things that you dislike doing within the program?

Matt—I dislike working with any sort of pen/brush in my hand. It’s just not for me but i have to get through the illustration stuff to finish this program. I really don’t like using the wacom tablets...
